About Me

Greetings! I am a Ph.D student in the Statistics Department at Iowa State University. Born in Tennessee and raised in Texas, I am now managing to survive in Iowa.



I completed my Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics and Bachelor of Science in Economics at the University of Houston in May of 2012. During my undergraduate career, I assisted Dr. Ruxandra Prodan with her research in long-run GDP trends across all 26 OECD countries. Utilizing advanced time series techniques, we tested the significance of structural breaks in growth: specifically, linear shifts, level shifts, or a combination of both. Conducting this research helped me to realize my love for statistics and desire to study this field at the graduate level.



In my spare time I enjoy eating, sleeping, and showering. In my extra spare time I enjoy writing, recording, and producing music at an amateur level. Whenever I accumulate large blocks of time and money, I like to travel the world. Check out my "Fun Stuff" section for examples of my music and memoirs from my globe-trotting endeavors.
